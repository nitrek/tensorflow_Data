POSTPONEMENT ORDER

The Securities and Exchange Commission instituted this proceeding with an Order
Instituting Proceedings (OIP) on June 18, 2014. The proceeding is a follow-on proceeding based on
SEC v. Pedras, No. 13-cv-7932 (C.D. Cal. June 9, 2014), in which Respondent Christopher A.T.
Pedras (a/k/a Chris Pedras a/k/a Antone Thomas Pedras), was enjoined, by default, against
violations of the antifraud and registration provisions of the federal securities laws. He has also
been indicted on eleven counts of wire fraud. United States v. Pedras, No. 8:13-cr-219 (C.D. Cal.
Aug. 6, 2014), ECF No. 11. Pedras, who was recently extradited from Tonga to face the criminal
charges, was served with the OIP on September 30, 2016, by personal service. His Answer to the
OIP is due by October 20, 2016. See OIP at 3; 17 C.F.R. § 201.220(b). The Division of
Enforcement has requested that the telephonic prehearing conference be postponed to a date after
the due date for his Answer. Pedras is incarcerated pending trial, currently set for November 29,
2016.1 United States v. Pedras, ECF Nos. 27, 30, 32.
IT IS ORDERED that the telephonic prehearing conference scheduled for October 14, 2016,
will be postponed sine die. The Division of Enforcement will report on a date or dates, on or after
November 14, 2016, when Pedras is available to participate in a telephonic prehearing conference.2
IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that this order and future orders and correspondence be served
on Pedras at his current address: Christopher A.T. Pedras, a/k/a Antone Thomas Pedras (Register
No. 74111-112), MDC Los Angeles, Metropolitan Detention Center, P.O. Box 1500, Los Angeles,
CA 90053.
