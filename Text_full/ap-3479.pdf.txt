In the Matter of

ORDER TO SHOW
CAUSE

YAYI INTERNATIONAL, INC.

On December 23, 2015, the Securities and Exchange Commission issued an order
instituting administrative proceedings (OIP) against Respondent pursuant to Section 12(j) of the
Securities Exchange Act of 1934. A telephonic prehearing conference is scheduled for January
26, 2016.
Respondent was served with the OIP on December 28, 2015, and its answer was due by
January 7, 2016. Yayi Int’l, Inc., Admin. Proc. Rulings Release No. 3468, 2016 SEC LEXIS 24
(Jan. 5, 2016). To date, Respondent has not filed an answer.
Accordingly, I ORDER that by January 19, 2016, Respondent shall SHOW CAUSE why
the registration of its securities should not be revoked by default due to its failure to file an
answer or otherwise defend this proceeding. See 17 C.F.R. §§ 201.155(a)(2), .220(f). If
Respondent fails to respond to this order, it will be deemed in default, the proceeding will be
determined against it, and the registration of its securities will be revoked. OIP at 2-3; 17 C.F.R.
§§ 201.155(a)(2), .220(f). The prehearing conference will take place on January 26, 2016, as
scheduled.
