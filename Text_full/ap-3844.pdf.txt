ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE

On March 8, 2016, the Securities and Exchange Commission issued an order instituting
administrative and cease-and-desist proceedings (OIP) against Respondents pursuant to Section 8A
of the Securities Act of 1933, Section 21C of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, Sections 203(e),
203(f), and 203(k) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940, and Section 9(b) of the Investment
Company Act of 1940. On April 12, 2016, the Division of Enforcement submitted certifications
which demonstrated that Respondents were personally served with the OIP by a process server on
April 7, 2016, in accordance with Commission Rule of Practice 141(a)(2)(i), (ii), 17 C.F.R. §
201.141(a)(2)(i), (ii).
Respondents’ answers were due May 11, 2016. Steven Zoernack, Admin. Proc. Rulings
Release No. 3756, 2016 SEC LEXIS 1214 (ALJ Apr. 1, 2016). By May 12, 2016, the parties were
required to hold a prehearing conference without the hearing officer, and they were to file a joint
prehearing conference statement by May 13. Id. To date, Respondents have not filed answers. On
May 13, 2016, the Division filed a unilateral prehearing conference statement establishing that,
despite notice, Respondents failed to participate in a prehearing conference or prepare a joint
prehearing conference statement.
Accordingly, Respondents are ORDERED to SHOW CAUSE by May 23, 2016, why this
proceeding should not be determined against them due to their failure to file answers or otherwise
defend the proceeding. If Respondents fail to respond to this order, they will be deemed in default.
OIP at 8; 17 C.F.R. §§ 201.155(a)(2), .220(f).
The parties are reminded to email electronic courtesy copies of any filings in this proceeding
in Word and PDF text-searchable format to ALJ@sec.gov.
