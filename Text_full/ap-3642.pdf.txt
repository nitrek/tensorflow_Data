ORDER TO SHOW
CAUSE

On February 4, 2016, the Securities and Exchange Commission issued an order
instituting proceedings (OIP) pursuant to Section 12(j) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934,
alleging that Respondents have securities registered with the Commission and are delinquent in
their periodic filings.1 A telephonic prehearing conference is scheduled for March 25, 2016, if
the proceeding is not resolved before then.
On February 10, 2016, I found that Respondents were served with the OIP by February 8
and that their answers were due by February 22, 2016. CBC Acquisition Corp. 1, Admin. Proc.
Rulings Release No. 3596, 2016 SEC LEXIS 500. To date, no Respondent has filed an answer.
Accordingly, it is ORDERED that Respondents CBC Acquisition Corp. 1, China
Renewable Construction Materials, Inc., and Whoopass Poker Corp. shall SHOW CAUSE by
March 7, 2016, why the registrations of their securities should not be revoked by default due to
their failure to file answers or otherwise defend this proceeding. If a Respondent fails to respond
to this order, it shall be deemed in default, the proceeding will be determined against it, and the
registration of its securities will be revoked. See OIP at 3; 17 C.F.R. §§ 201.155(a)(2), .220(f).
