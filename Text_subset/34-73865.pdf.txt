On December 18, 2013, the Securities and Exchange Commission (“Commission”)
issued an order (“Order”) finding that G-Trade Services LLC, ConvergEx Global Markets
Limited, and ConvergEx Execution Solutions LLC (“Respondents”) violated the federal
securities laws.1 Pursuant to the Order, Respondents paid disgorgement of $79,802,448,
prejudgment interest of $7,621,981, and a civil money penalty of $20,000,000 for a total of
$107,424,429. The Order created a Fair Fund pursuant to Section 308(a) of the Sarbanes-Oxley
Act of 2002, as amended. The Fair Fund includes the $107,424,429 paid by Respondents, plus

The Division of Enforcement now seeks the appointment of The Garden City Group
(“GCG”) as the fund plan administrator and requests that the administrator’s bond be set at
$108,653,021, as required by Rules 1105(a) and 1105(c) of the Commission’s Rules on Fair
Fund and Disgorgement Plans (“Rules”).3 GCG is included in the Commission’s approved pool
of administrators.
Accordingly, pursuant to Rule 1105(a), it is hereby ORDERED that GCG is appointed as
the fund plan administrator and shall obtain a bond in accordance with Rule 1105(c) in the
amount of $108,653,021.
